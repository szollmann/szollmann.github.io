Welcome to the Visual Computing Aarhus Lab

We are a research group within the Human-Centered Computing Section at the Department of Computer Science at Aarhus University. Our work, led by Associate Professor Stefanie Zollmann, operates at the intersection of computer graphics, computer vision, human-computer interaction (HCI), and visualization.

Our mission is to seamlessly blend the real and virtual worlds, exploring how technology can enhance human perception and interaction with data and the surrounding environment. We have a focus on creating immersive and interactive experiences using eXtended Reality (XR), Augmented Reality (AR), and Virtual Reality (VR).

üî¨ Research Focus

Our group explores several key themes within visual computing. We are particularly interested in:

  • Situated Visualization: We research and develop techniques to display digital data and visualizations directly in their real-world spatial context.
  • AR/VR for Sports and Media: We are actively developing new ways to use AR to enhance the experience of on-site sports spectators, providing real-time statistics and visualizations, as well as tools for immersive coaching and training.
  • Rendering Techniques for Immersive Experiences: We explore advanced rendering methods‚Äîincluding neural rendering, view synthesis, and view-dependent effects, to enhance realism and coherence in interactive XR environments.
